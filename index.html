ABOUT ME

Margieta Tandoc R.N., B.S.N.
IRF/PAI COORDINATOR

E-mail: margieta.tandoc@uphs.upenn.edu
Office Phone: 215-893-2576

I was born in the Philippines, where I had my primary and secondary education with my parent’s guidance, motivation, and emphasis that education is a valuable wealth. I started my nursing education at the University of Pangasinan, Philippines, and graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Nursing. I expanded my nursing experience, knowledge, and skills by working in Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and finally, the United States of America.

In working with different healthcare systems and diverse population in various countries, I have learned that I can contribute further in other fields of nursing. There are emerging changes in the process of teaching and learning which could improve and produce better outcomes in healthcare.

I found out that I could do more by becoming an instrument for new graduates’ and young nurses’ nursing education. To achieve this, I am pursuing my Masters in Education with a specialization in Nursing Education.

Today, as an Inpatient Rehabilitation Facility-Payment Assessment Instrument (IRF-PAI) Coordinator, I coordinate with the interdisciplinary teams in achieving quality and safety of patient care through proper documentation and provision of data required by the Centers of Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS). I am currently working with a team of quality and patient risk management.

I am looking forward to contributing better health outcomes by working collaboratively with the team, and continue assisting with documentation training of nurses, therapists, and other staff. This e-portfolio could serve as a guideline on how employers could find me in the field of nursing education through displaying my theoretical basis for nursing practice, statement of professional goals, and resume.

Statement of Professional Goals

Short and Long Term

At present, my short-term goals are revolving around my current position as an IRF-PAI Coordinator and a team member in the quality and patient risk management, by giving me an opportunity to practice my skills in maintaining quality and safety of patients through proper documentation and identifying the quality indicators and effective measurement of quality outcomes from the data collected from the interdisciplinary staff. In addition, the Functional Independence Monitoring (FIM) training of nurses and therapists is another task that I do in my current position. These tasks may lead to the development of my short-term goals to continue maintaining quality, and safety until time has come to achieve my long-term goals in nursing education. .

My long-term goals may include effective teaching in a healthcare setting or online setting, with emphasis on assisting with creative distance based learning courses. My background in working from multiple countries, and my various health care setting orientations may support another goal in teaching students about transcultural healthcare or engage with any curriculum that could help students learn diverse cultural healthcare. In addition, my long-term goal is to teach simulation courses in a classroom or online. Along with these goals may include the maintenance of quality and safety in any tasks that I need to achieve in becoming a nursing educator.
